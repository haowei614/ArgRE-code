# Blind DJS Sub-Study — Rater Instructions

## Task

You will rate a set of software requirements drawn from three case studies
(Autonomous Driving, ATM, Bookkeeping). For each item, assign a Decision
Justification Score (DJS) from 1 to 5.

In this study, you are shown **only the final requirement text**. No
supporting artifacts (argumentation graphs, trace cards, negotiation logs, or
method labels) are provided for any item. Base your judgment solely on the
visible text. Do not attempt to infer which method or system produced an item.

## DJS Scale (1–5)

- **5 — Fully Justified.** Complete, self-contained explanation. Every step
  from proposal through critique, refinement, and resolution is explicit.
- **4 — Mostly Justified.** Clear explanation with minor gaps. Main reasoning
  is clear but 1–2 intermediate steps require slight inference.
- **3 — Partially Justified.** Broad understanding of acceptance, but
  significant parts of reasoning are implicit or require guesswork.
- **2 — Weakly Justified.** Minimal justification. You know the requirement
  was accepted but cannot reconstruct the reasoning.
- **1 — No Justification.** No information about why this requirement was
  accepted over alternatives. Output is a black box.

This is the same scale used in the earlier DJS evaluation. If the visible
text alone does not allow you to reconstruct why the requirement was accepted,
score accordingly; do not assume the existence of supporting material you
cannot see.

## Procedure

1. Open your assigned sheet (`blind_djs_sheet_R1.csv`, `_R2.csv`, or
   `_R3.csv`). Item order differs between raters; this is intentional.
2. Work through the items in the order given. For each item, fill in
   `djs_score_1_to_5` (integer 1–5) and, optionally, a brief `comment`.
3. Rate each item independently. Do not revise earlier scores after seeing
   later items, and do not discuss items with other raters until all sheets
   are returned.
4. The `case_study` column is provided only as context for what kind of
   system the requirement belongs to.

There are 13 items. Expected time: 15–20 minutes.
